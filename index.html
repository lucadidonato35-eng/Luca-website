The Photographer

A Lens Held
to the World

Luca Di Donato has spent the better part of a decade moving through remote places with a camera, a notebook, and the patience that wild subjects demand. Born in Milan, drawn outward.

His work lives at the intersection of wildlife documentary and portraiture — the moment before language, where instinct and light say everything. He has tracked big cats across Botswana's Okavango Delta, documented ancient traditions in Vietnam's northern highlands, and waited in silence for light that arrives only once.

From the fjords of Norway to the coral atolls of the Maldives, from Spain's sierras to the streets of Seoul — Luca shoots exclusively in natural light. The work is quiet. It asks to be looked at slowly.
